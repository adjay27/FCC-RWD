SOEKARNO

Founders of Indonesia

1901 - 1970

Ir. Soekarno Speech in front of Indonesian people

Ir. Soekarno, the first President of Indonesia, had a significant impact on the country's history. Here are some key achievements and details about him:

  • 1901 - Soekarno is born on June 6, in Surabaya, East Java, Dutch East Indies (now Indonesia).
  • 1926 - Soekarno studied civil engineering at the Technische Hoogeschool (TH) in Bandung, Dutch East Indies (now Bandung Institute of Technology or ITB). He graduated in 1926 with the degree of Ingenieur (Ir.), which is equivalent to a bachelor's degree in engineering.
  • 1927 - Soekarno establishes the Indonesian National Party (Partai Nasional Indonesia or PNI) and becomes its leader. He begins to actively advocate for Indonesian independence.
  • 1942-1945 - Indonesia is occupied by the Japanese during World War II. Soekarno initially cooperates with the Japanese authorities, hoping for support in gaining independence.
  • 1945 - On August 17, just days after Japan's surrender, Soekarno and Mohammad Hatta proclaim the independence of Indonesia. Soekarno becomes the first President of the Republic of Indonesia.
  • 1949 - The Dutch, who had tried to reassert control over Indonesia after World War II, recognize Indonesia's independence following diplomatic negotiations.
  • 1955 - Soekarno convenes the Asia-Africa Conference in Bandung, Indonesia. The conference aims to promote Afro-Asian economic and cultural cooperation and is a significant event in the history of the Non-Aligned Movement.
  • 1956 - Soekarno introduces the concept of "Guided Democracy," which centralizes political power in the presidency. This marks a shift toward a more authoritarian style of government.
  • 1963 - Indonesia annexes Western New Guinea (now Papua) from Dutch control after a controversial and conflict-ridden process.
  • 1965 - A failed coup attempt, attributed to communists, occurs in Indonesia. This event leads to anti-communist purges, and Soekarno's power diminishes. General Suharto emerges as a prominent military leader.
  • 1967 - Soekarno is gradually sidelined, and on March 12, he formally transfers executive power to General Suharto. Soekarno remains under house arrest until his death.
  • 1970 - Soekarno passes away on June 21, in Jakarta, Indonesia.
Soekarno's legacy is complex, with supporters praising his role in achieving independence and promoting Third World unity, while critics highlight his authoritarian tendencies and the tumultuous events surrounding his later years in power.
